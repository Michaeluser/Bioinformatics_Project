POST-ALIGNMENT QUALITY CONTROL REPORT

This report summarizes alignment statistics for control (CS) and tumor (TS) samples following alignment to the reference human genome.

================================================================================
SAMPLE-SPECIFIC ALIGNMENT STATISTICS
================================================================================

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
CONTROL SAMPLE (CS)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Total reads: 23,948,283
The dataset contains ~24 million reads, indicating sufficient sequencing depth for downstream analysis.

Mapping rate: 99.97%
An extremely high proportion of reads successfully aligned to the reference genome. This indicates:
- High sequencing quality
- Minimal contamination
- Good compatibility between reads and reference genome

Properly paired reads: 99.23%
The vast majority of paired-end reads are correctly aligned with expected orientation and insert size. This suggests:
- Accurate library preparation
- Reliable structural integrity of reads

Singletons: 0.03%
A negligible fraction of reads have only one mate aligned. This low value indicates minimal fragmentation or mapping inconsistency.

Reads mapped to different chromosomes: 87,956 (~0.37%)
Reads whose pairs map to different chromosomes are present at a low level. After filtering for mapping quality (mapQ >= 5), this reduces to 76,711.
This may indicate:
- Minor structural variation
- Alignment ambiguity in repetitive regions
- Low-level technical noise

Secondary alignments: 18,591
A small number of reads map to multiple locations, which is expected in repetitive genomic regions.

Duplicates: 0
No duplicates reported at this stage (likely not yet marked). Duplicate marking should be performed in downstream processing.

Overall interpretation:
The control sample demonstrates excellent alignment quality, with high mapping efficiency, strong pairing consistency, and minimal anomalies. The dataset is suitable for downstream analyses such as variant calling or expression analysis.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
TUMOR SAMPLE (TS)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Total reads: 34,639,454
The tumor dataset contains ~34.6 million reads, providing higher sequencing depth than the control.

Mapping rate: 99.98%
Alignment success is extremely high, confirming:
- High read quality
- Strong compatibility with the reference genome

Properly paired reads: 99.34%
A very high proportion of reads are properly paired, slightly higher than in the control sample. This indicates robust alignment performance.

Singletons: 0.02%
Very low number of singletons, suggesting minimal mapping inconsistencies.

Reads mapped to different chromosomes: 83,864 (~0.24%)
After quality filtering (mapQ >= 5), this reduces to 71,073.
Compared to the control sample, this value is slightly lower, but still within expected range. These reads may reflect:

- Alignment ambiguity

Secondary alignments: 16,136
A modest number of multi-mapped reads, consistent with genomic repeats.

Duplicates: 0
No duplicates reported at this stage. Duplicate marking is required in later processing steps.

Overall interpretation:
The tumor sample exhibits excellent alignment metrics, with slightly improved pairing statistics compared to the control. High mapping rates and low singleton fractions confirm strong data quality. Minor interchromosomal mappings may reflect biological variation or alignment ambiguity.

================================================================================
COMPARATIVE SUMMARY
================================================================================

- Both samples show extremely high mapping rates (>99.9%), indicating high-quality sequencing data.
- Proper pairing is consistently above 99%, confirming reliable library preparation and alignment.
- Singleton rates are negligible in both datasets.
- Interchromosomal mappings are low and comparable between samples.
- No duplicates are reported yet, suggesting duplicate marking has not been performed.

Conclusion:
Both control and tumor datasets demonstrate high-quality alignment suitable for downstream bioinformatic analyses. Observed minor anomalies are within expected limits and do not indicate significant technical issues. Further validation using post-alignment QC tools (e.g., Qualimap) is recommended to assess coverage uniformity, GC bias, and genomic distribution.

================================================================================
SEQUENCE BIAS AND BASE QUALITY
================================================================================

The mapped reads nucleotide content profile shows overall stability with expected technical artifacts at the beginning of reads.

Initial fluctuations:
Noticeable variability in nucleotide percentages is observed within the first 10 base pairs.
This effect is typical and is attributed to non-random hexamer priming during library preparation.

Long-term stability:
After position 10 bp, nucleotide composition stabilizes across the entire read length (~100 bp).
Adenine (A) and Thymine (T) remain at approximately 26-27%, while Guanine (G) and Cytosine (C) remain at 23-24%.

Base-calling confidence:
The proportion of undefined bases (N) is 0% across all positions, indicating very high base-calling accuracy.

================================================================================
DUPLICATION PROFILE
================================================================================

The duplication rate histogram reveals the distribution and nature of duplicated reads.

Overall duplication rate:
30.22%, corresponding to approximately 10.8 million duplicated reads.

Primary distribution:
The majority of genomic loci (~16.5 million) have a duplication rate of 1, meaning they are covered by a single unique read.

Decay pattern:
The duplication frequency decreases rapidly:
- ~5 million loci have duplication rate = 2
- <1.5 million loci have duplication rate = 3

Interpretation:
The exponential decay pattern indicates that duplicates are broadly distributed rather than concentrated.
This strongly suggests PCR amplification artifacts rather than biological over-representation or contamination.
